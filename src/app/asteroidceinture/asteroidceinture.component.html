Trojan asteroids are not a belt, but a group of asteroids. They share the orbit of the planet Jupiter around the Sun near Lagrange points L4 and L5 of the orbit of this planet, namely they are located at 60° before or after Jupiter. The Lagrange point, noted from L1 to L5, is a position where the gravity fields of two bodies orbitally moving around each other provide the exact centripetal force (e.g. a force maintaining an object in a curved trajectory) required for this point of space to simultaneously follow the orbital motion of the two bodies. They are called Trojans because of a convention that names them after the characters of the Trojan War. The asteroids at point L4 are named after a hero or a concept and are called "Greek camp". Those in point L5 bear the names of Trojan heroes and are collectively named "Trojan camp".
